The Art of Living

If there's one person who has genuinely changed the way I see life, it's Laiba. She's my best friend, my person, and honestly one of the most quietly inspiring people I know. This is my first blog post, and I couldn't think of a better subject than…her. ✨

Lesson 1: Dream Big, Even When It's Scary

Laiba has these massive dreams. We planned on going to Turkey, to Korea, to Italy, and whatnot. And yeah, we both know we are far from that life right now, but we've got this little ball of joy; it never lets those ideas down. She taught me how to dream, to think of a future that is so far… but if you have enough enthusiasm and imagination, you can make it true one day. Most people would've let it down, saying it's wishful thinking, but with her… anything is possible. She never lets the uncertainty shrink what she wants. She reminded me that a practical life is important, but a life with zero dreams in it? That sounds exhausting. So dream big, even when you don't know how it's going to work out yet.

Lesson 2: Be Fully Present

This one I'm still learning from her. Laiba has this way of being completely there, in conversations, in small moments, in the in-between parts of life that most of us rush through. She'll sit with you and actually be with you. Through her I've learned that the present moment is the only one we actually have, and it deserves our full attention. When we’re together its the most seen Ive ever felt. She would look at you and it would feel like you are the most important person to her and that’s in so many levels is very inspiring.

Lesson 3: Show Up For Your People

It's really that simple. When things get hard, she doesn't disappear; she stays. Like this time on my 16th birthday I had this party in my house; she was the first one to show up, and that day everything went bad. Anything I could’ve ever imagined, and in that moment I closed off; I acted like the biggest jerk person ever, but instead of her being mad or sad or feeling any way, she took care of everything. She became this wave of joy, this person who can somehow fix everything and still be so joyful. Since then I knew if something goes wrong I have a person with me who can magically fix it all. She's taught me that relationships aren't built in the big grand gestures; they're built in the consistent, quiet act of being there. That's something I want to carry with me always.

I'm lucky to have someone like her in my corner. And if this post does anything, I hope it reminds you to hold your people a little closer and maybe tell them what they mean to you before another day passes. So yeah, Laiba, I don’t know if you ever read this, but if you do, thank you so much for those quiet moments when you were there for me. When you held me while everything was a mess. When I didn’t know what the next day would be about, you taught me today mattered as much as the next day.
